Concept 05 — Guided Wizard

This concept acknowledges that the hardest moment is setup, not steady-state use. Instead of dumping the user into a form, the popup acts like a short assistant: enable the feature, connect the API, choose a model, and confirm readiness. Only after that does the compact steady-state UI appear.

After setup, the popup collapses into a simpler “ready / detected / off” interface. Wizard UI is for onboarding and recovery only.

Options become setup summary, not raw form

The options page mirrors the wizard outcome. It reads like a checklist of what is configured, what can be edited, and what still needs attention. That makes the product less intimidating for first-time users.

Why it differs
The same product has distinct onboarding and steady-state layouts. The popup is not static across the lifecycle.
Best fit
Users who are unsure how OpenRouter, models, and automated skipping relate to each other.
